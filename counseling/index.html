Welcome to a Safe Space — No Wipes Here

For over twenty years, MythicProgress.org Marriage Counseling has served as the premier relationship support service for couples navigating the unique challenges that arise when one or both partners are dedicated members of the World of Warcraft raiding community.

We founded this practice in 2005 after our lead therapist, Dr. Karen Lightbane, noticed a disturbing pattern: fully 40% of her clients cited "excessive gaming" as a primary relationship stressor, and of those, 73% specifically named World of Warcraft. Rather than treating raid commitment as a pathology, Dr. Lightbane chose to understand it — and MythicProgress.org was born.

Our approach is unique: we don't ask raiders to stop raiding. We don't ask partners to "just deal with it." Instead, we equip both parties with the communication tools they need to negotiate raid schedules, manage loot-related emotional triggers, and build a relationship that can survive even the longest progression tiers.

Is Your Relationship in Need of Mythic-Level Intervention?

A free self-assessment developed by our clinical team

Sometimes it's hard to tell whether your challenges are normal or whether they've crossed into territory requiring professional support. We developed this assessment from twenty years of clinical experience with the raiding community.

Read each statement and keep count of how many apply to your household.

Be honest. There's no enrage timer on self-reflection.

1
Has your partner referred to dinner as "a bio break"?
2
Does your partner describe your relationship issues as "mechanics we need to learn"?
3
Has your partner ever said "I can't, we're progressing" when asked to attend a family event, date night, or their child's recital?
4
Do they refer to couples therapy as "wiping on communication mechanics"?
5
Has your partner described your mother-in-law as "an add that needs to be dealt with"?
6
Do they say "that's not in my rotation" when asked to do household chores?
7
Has your partner ever whispered "releasing spirit" when falling asleep on the couch?
8
Do they describe getting a raise at work as "finally getting a loot drop"?
9
When you cry during an argument, do they instinctively say "I'll dispel that"?
10
Has your partner called in sick to work on a patch day more than twice this year?
11
Do they evaluate potential babysitters based on whether the sitter's availability conflicts with raid nights?
12
Has your partner ever said "we need to optimize our throughput" during a conversation about dividing household responsibilities?
13
When you suggest a vacation, do they ask if the hotel has "acceptable latency"?
14
Have they ever described leaving a social gathering early as "hearthing out"?
15
Has your partner proposed to you, or discussed major life decisions, in between boss pulls?

Meet Our Clinical Team

Licensed professionals who understand both attachment theory and threat tables.

Dr. Karen Lightbane, PhD, LMFT

Founder & Clinical Director — Resto Shaman (Retired)

Dr. Lightbane founded MythicProgress.org in 2005 after recognizing a gap in traditional couples therapy: therapists who dismissed gaming as "just a phase" were failing an entire generation of couples. With a PhD in Clinical Psychology from UC Davis and 10 years of raiding experience (including server-first Ragnaros), she brings a unique dual perspective. She retired from active raiding in 2019 to "spend more time with her husband," which she notes is "ironic, but the right call."

Dr. James Tankswell, PsyD, LPC

Senior Therapist & Raid-Night Crisis Specialist — Protection Paladin (Active, 2-night Heroic)

Dr. Tankswell specializes in couples where one partner is a guild officer or raid leader, which he understands comes with unique interpersonal pressures. "Leading 20 people through Mythic content while your spouse texts you about the dishwasher is a very specific kind of stress," he says. He maintains a strict 2-night raid schedule his wife helped negotiate — a process he now teaches to clients.

Sarah Moonwell, MSW, LCSW

Couples Therapist & Non-Gamer Liaison — "I don't play, and that's the point"

Sarah is the only member of our clinical team who has never played WoW. This is intentional. "I represent the partner who doesn't get it," she says. "When a non-gaming partner feels unheard, I'm the one in the room who genuinely understands that perspective — because I've lived it. My husband has raided since Burning Crusade." Her specialty is translating between "gamer-speak" and "feelings-speak" in real time.
